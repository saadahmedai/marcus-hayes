The Niche Bending Cheat Sheet

The core formula that makes faceless YouTube channels work. Understand this fully before moving to any other system. This is where everything begins.

Market + Format = Your Bent Niche

A Market is your topic. A Format is your video style. Combine a proven viral format from one market with a completely fresh market — and you create content that has never existed before.

🎯

What is a Market?

A stable topic with an established audience. Pick something you genuinely know and find interesting.

  • History & ancient civilizations
  • Animals & wildlife
  • True crime & mysteries
  • Personal finance & investing
  • Psychology & behavior
  • Technology & AI
📐

What is a Format?

The video delivery structure — borrowed from a different market where it already went viral.

  • "Every [X] Explained" series
  • Ranking/tier list format
  • "If [X] does [Y], it means…"
  • True crime suspense structure
  • Documentary expose style
  • 100-day challenge format
⚡

The Bend Rule

The format must be proven viral in a different market than yours. The more distance between the two, the more original your content feels.

Gaming tier lists → applied to zoology = blue ocean. Gaming tier lists → applied to another gaming topic = crowded.

🌍

Target High-RPM Countries

Design every video specifically for USA, UK, Canada, and Australia. These countries pay 5–15× more per view in ad revenue.

  • English-language content only
  • American or British AI voice
  • Upload 9 AM–2 PM Eastern Time
  • Set channel location: United States
🚫

The #1 Mistake

Competing directly with an existing channel using the same topic and the same format. YouTube puts your video head-to-head with theirs. You will almost always lose.

Your idea must be original. Only the format structure is borrowed — never the topic.

🛡️

Stay Safe from AI Policy

YouTube's 2026 "inauthentic content" policy deleted thousands of channels. Always add your human editorial layer — fact-check, reframe, and personalize every script before publishing.

AI is the assistant. You are the creator. That distinction is what YouTube tests for.
