01

Two Years of Trying Things That Didn't Work

I'm from Fresno, California. At 22 I decided I didn't want a regular job, so I started trying online business models. First was affiliate marketing — I built a blog, wrote for months, and got almost no traffic. Then dropshipping, which cost me $400 and returned one sale in three months.

Then YouTube. I launched three faceless channels between 2022 and 2024. The first one got 80 subscribers before I gave up on it. The second got a little further. The third hit 200 and flatlined. I kept making videos nobody watched, with no real idea why they weren't working.

By late 2024 I had spent two years and had very little to show for it. I came close to stopping completely.

📸 marcus-early.jpg 800 × 600px Marcus looking tired or frustrated at a laptop, late night setting.
02

Finding the Framework That Actually Made Sense

In early 2025 I came across the concept of Niche Bending. The idea was straightforward — instead of picking an already crowded topic and hoping to compete, you combine a format that's already proven viral in one category with a completely different market where nobody has used it yet.

That was different from everything I'd tried before. I wasn't competing with existing channels anymore. I was putting together something that didn't exist yet, but that had all the ingredients of something people already watched.

Script Bending worked the same way — I took the structural framework of a viral script from one niche and applied it to a completely original topic. Not copying. Not reusing. Just using what already worked as a blueprint.

The other part that changed things was targeting. I'd been making videos for a general audience. The Niche Bending system is specifically designed for USA, UK, Canada, and Australia — countries where advertisers pay significantly more. That alone changed how much each 1,000 views was worth.

"I wasn't looking for a loophole. I was looking for something that made logical sense — and this was the first thing that did."

Market + Format = Untapped Niche
Target USA/UK = 8× higher RPM
03

The First Time It Actually Worked

I launched a new Shorts channel in January 2025. No face. No microphone recording. AI visuals from Google Flow, voice generated through ElevenLabs, edited in CapCut. Three tools.

I applied the Niche Bending formula for the topic, used a Script Bending framework for the structure, and produced content specifically designed to attract viewers in the USA and UK.

Fourteen days in, one video hit 3 million views. By day 30 the channel had crossed 20 million total views. The YouTube Partner Program approved the channel that same month.

The first AdSense payment arrived in my account shortly after. It wasn't a massive number. But it was more than I'd made from two years of everything else combined. That was enough to know this was real.

Why This Exists

Why I Started Teaching This

It's not complicated — but it does need to be explained properly

When I look back at the two years I spent trying things that didn't work, most of the mistakes came down to not having a clear system. I was making decisions based on what seemed like good ideas at the time, not on a framework that had been tested.

Niche Bending isn't a secret or a hack. It's a logical way of approaching content that reduces the amount of guessing involved. Most people who fail at faceless YouTube fail because they're competing in spaces that are already full, targeting audiences that don't pay well, and producing content without a repeatable process.

I built these systems because I wanted to document what I actually did — not a cleaned-up version, not a course that skips the difficult parts. The three systems cover the full path from understanding the method to building income from it, in that order.

If you go through System 1 and it doesn't make sense to you, that's fine. Not every approach works for every person. But if it does make sense, everything you need to act on it is in the next two systems.
