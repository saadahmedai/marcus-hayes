Two Years of Trying Everything

I grew up in Fresno, California. At 22, I decided I was done with the idea of a regular 9-to-5 and started trying everything online. Affiliate marketing first — built a blog, wrote articles for months. Nobody read them. Then dropshipping — lost $400, got one sale in three months.

I launched three YouTube channels over 18 months. All three failed. The last one hit 200 subscribers and flatlined completely. I was embarrassed. I almost quit everything entirely.

📸marcus-early.jpg
02

The Strategy That Changed Everything

In early 2025 I discovered Niche Bending — instead of picking an overcrowded topic and hoping to compete, you combine a proven viral format from one category with a completely fresh market nobody has touched yet. You engineer the niche instead of guessing.

I also learned Script Bending — using known viral storytelling frameworks applied to completely original ideas. Suddenly I wasn't competing with anyone. I was creating something that didn't exist yet but that people clearly wanted to watch.

And critically, I learned to target USA, UK, Canada, and Australia — the countries advertisers pay the most for. That alone changed my earnings per 1,000 views by 8×.

"What if the niche doesn't have to exist yet — you just create it?"

Market + Format = Untapped Niche 🎯
Target USA/UK = 8× higher RPM 💰
03

The Month Everything Changed

I launched a new Shorts channel in January 2025. No face. No microphone. Just AI visuals from Google Flow, AI voice from ElevenLabs, edited in CapCut. Three tools. Nothing else.

I applied Niche Bending for the topic, Script Bending for the format, and specifically designed content for USA and UK audiences. Two weeks in, one video hit 3 million views. By day 30, the channel had over 20 million total views and I was approved for the YouTube Partner Program.

The first payment was more than I'd made in a month at my last part-time job. I sat in my apartment and just stared at the number. I cried, honestly.
